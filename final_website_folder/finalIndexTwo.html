Your Heart Pounds In Your Chest

it presses against the discarded rock, twisting a turning as you continue to try and inspire energy into the rest of your body. after what you can only assume to be a few minutes of needless struggle and lost oxygen, you begin to come to terms with the fact that only your head and torso seem functional. you begin to thrash harder, as the rubble cascades down your form. you greedily gulp in gasps of air and cough dryly in your fervent desperation. blaring heat hits your aching face while you lay there. motionless.
